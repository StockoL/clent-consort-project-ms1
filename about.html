We believe in the power of historic spaces to transform choral music.

Our mission is to bridge the gap between ancient stone and modern voice.

A Note from the Conductor

"At The Clent Consort, we believe that choral music is not a static art form, but one that lives and breathes within the architecture for which it was written. Our approach is intentionally different from the traditional weekly rehearsal model.

By operating on a project-based cycle, we allow our singers - some of whom are professional musicians and some dedicated amateurs—to immerse themselves intensely in a specific era or liturgical theme. We don't just 'perform' in cathedrals; we inhabit them. This model ensures that when we stand in the stalls of a cathedral, a great abbey, or a hidden medieval chapel, every vowel and every rest is tuned to the specific resonance of those ancient stones. It is a demanding process, but one that yields a profound, singular musical identity."
