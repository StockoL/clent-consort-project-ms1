We believe in the power of historic spaces to transform choral music.

Our mission is to bridge the gap between ancient stone and modern voice.

A Note from the Conductor

"At The Clent Consort, we believe that choral music is not a static art form, but one that lives and breathes within the architecture for which it was written. Our approach is intentionally different from the traditional weekly rehearsal model.

By operating on a project-based cycle, we allow our singers - some of whom are professional musicians and some dedicated amateurs—to immerse themselves intensely in a specific era or liturgical theme. We don't just 'perform' in cathedrals; we inhabit them. This model ensures that when we stand in the stalls of a cathedral, a great abbey, or a hidden medieval chapel, every vowel and every rest is tuned to the specific resonance of those ancient stones. It is a demanding process, but one that yields a profound, singular musical identity."

Our Project Rhythm

1. Foundations

Every project begins with the space. We identify cathedrals and historic foundations we would like to visit, aiming to perform in new places regularly. Once the residency is secured, we ascertain the availability of the Consort's singers for the project.

2. Preparation

Music is then chosen to best suit the space and the ensemble. Preparation is a dual process of rehearsal and research. Singers receive scores and high-fidelity rehearsal tracks weeks in advance, allowing our limited face-to-face time to be spent as much as possible on blend, tuning, and 'liturgical flow' rather than note-learning. We study the historical context of the music to ensure our interpretation is both technically flawless and spiritually informed.

3. Residency

The cycle culminates in a residency — either a full weekend of choral services, or a Saturday Evensong. Living and singing together in the shadow of the cathedral, the ensemble achieves a level of 'consort-identity' that only comes through shared focus. Whether it is a solemn Evensong or a festal Eucharist, the residency is where the preparation meets the stone, creating a unique moment of musical transcendence.
